## Course Description

This service-learning course examines the roles, values, and objectives of public and non-profit organizations in San Diego working at the intersections of race and criminal justice. Students gain hands-on experience through community service that directly supports justice-involved individuals in the greater San Diego area.

Through the lens of community-driven research, students learn strategies for informing smarter, more equitable programs and policies. As a senior practicum course, coursework is explicitly connected to transferable professional skills to support students' career goals.



## Course Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, students will be able to:

1. Conceptualize critical components of a functioning community
2. Explore the many meanings of "community" and "community resources"
3. Synthesize how the criminal justice system affects different communities
4. Comprehend the role of different branches of the criminal justice system and identify resources available to those impacted by the system
5. Identify justice system "clients" and their needs
6. Work with diverse others on issues of common concern

## Assignments

### Course Preparation Module (45 pts)
Completed during Week 1. Includes verifying the email policy, setting up Google Doc submissions, updating your course profile, and posting a video introduction on the class discussion board.

### Course Engagement (50 pts)
Regular, consistent engagement with course content is essential. Engagement is assessed through active participation and logging into Canvas to access course materials throughout the semester.

### Service Hours Timesheet (30 pts)
Students log their service hours in an ongoing timesheet throughout the semester, tracking time devoted to projects connected to the California Racial Justice Act (CRJA).

### Video Journal Entries (25 pts — 5 entries)
Five brief video journal entries recorded throughout the semester. Each entry should address a topic covered in the course or reflect on your service experience, with a focus on themes related to the CRJA.

### Final Group Presentation (50 pts)
A culminating group presentation on students' data and findings related to racial disparities, delivered during finals week.
